03  /  Approach

Why the combination matters

Most BIM coordinators come from architecture. They can flag a clash — two objects overlapping in a model — but they cannot tell you whether it matters, or what correcting it involves on site.

I come from the site. With a background spanning carpentry, structural engineering, and MEP electrical work, I assess what a clash means in construction reality: whether the structural member can be modified, what the load path consequence is, and what the fix will cost if left until the build phase. That chain of reasoning is what a project manager actually needs from a coordination report.

→
Structural depth. Load path implications assessed for every structural clash — not just geometry.
→
MEP knowledge. Duct, pipe, and conduit conflicts resolved with understanding of system constraints — not just clearance rules.
→
Site-built. 15 years on Nordic construction sites. What "fix it on site" actually costs is not an abstraction.
